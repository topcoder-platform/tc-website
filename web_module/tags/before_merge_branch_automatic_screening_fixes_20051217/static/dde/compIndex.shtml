Purchasing Components
Distributed Development

The enterprise software landscape can be thought of as a spectrum. On one end of the spectrum are software packages (SAP, Siebel, PeopleSoft, etc.). The other end of the spectrum consists of customized software, written by in-house developers, consultants or a combination of the two. The primary benefit of a packaged solution is that since the costs of development are borne by a number of companies, users of the package are able to get functionality at less than the cost of a custom solution. Experience has shown, however, that packaged solutions are by nature difficult to implement and costly to modify due to their proprietary and monolithic nature.

The opposite end of the spectrum is custom software development. The primary benefit of a custom solution is that a customer can expect to receive a product that is tailored exactly to their needs. Custom development has become increasingly expensive due to the high cost of quality software developers and the lack of leverage in custom software development.

Since neither end of the spectrum allows a company to get what they need at the price they desire, progressive companies have begun to take a new approach: Component Based Development (CBD). CBD gives a company the best of both worlds. Companies can use what are essentially pieces of applications that can be customized and tied together for their particular use. Cost savings are achieved through a market approach to component development. Each component is sold to multiple companies thereby distributing development costs.

This shift in the spectrum provides an opportunity for suppliers of reusable, component based solutions. Component Based Development (CBD) allows for applications to be built by customizing and integrating infrastructure and business components into enterprise applications.
